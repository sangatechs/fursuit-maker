Sizing Guide — New York Events

A trustworthy fursuit maker will guide sizing with clear measurement guides and fit checkpoints; accurate sizing ensures comfort during New York events, better movement, and longer wear without hotspots or chafing.

The Key Measurements Every Maker Needs
What you measure and why it matters for your New York con fit
📏
Measurement 01
Head Circumference
Measured at the widest point around the head. This determines the foam base size and is the most critical measurement for head comfort during long New York con days.
👕
Measurement 02
Chest and Waist
Measured at the fullest point while breathing normally. The bodysuit needs enough room for ventilation without being so loose that fabric bunches and causes chafing during prolonged movement.
📐
Measurement 03
Torso Length
From shoulder to hip at front and back. Ensures the bodysuit sits correctly without riding up during walking, bending, or posing — critical for New York cons where you are active for hours.
🦵
Measurement 04
Inseam and Hip
Hip circumference and inseam determine leg panel sizing. Wrong inseam creates a pulling sensation across the hips during walking that becomes intensely uncomfortable across a full convention day.
🦶
Measurement 05
Foot Length and Width
Paw pads built to the wrong foot size create the most immediate comfort problem — you feel it with every step across a New York convention floor. Measure both feet as they often differ slightly.
✋
Measurement 06
Hand and Wrist
Mitten width at knuckles and wrist circumference ensure hand paws fit securely without sliding and can be removed quickly when you need to manage personal items or take a heat break.

What Good vs Poor Fit Feels Like at a New York Con

The difference between a well-fitted fursuit and one that is even slightly off becomes dramatically apparent over a full convention day. New York cons involve walking miles of convention floor, posing for dozens of photos, navigating crowds, and maintaining your character for hours. A suit that fits correctly becomes part of you during all of this; a suit with fit problems becomes the thing you are fighting against constantly instead of inhabiting comfortably.

✓ Well-Fitted Suit
  • ✓ Head sits level without tipping
  • ✓ Arms move fully without pulling
  • ✓ No fabric bunching at joints
  • ✓ Paws stay in place during wear
  • ✓ Comfortable for 2+ hours continuously
  • ✓ No persistent pressure points
✗ Poorly-Fitted Suit
  • ✗ Head rocks or sits low
  • ✗ Arm raise pulls torso up
  • ✗ Fabric folds dig into skin
  • ✗ Paws rotate or slide during wear
  • ✗ Discomfort builds quickly
  • ✗ Red marks or chafing on skin

Common Hotspot Locations and How Makers Address Them

Fit Checkpoints During Your Commission

A trustworthy New York fursuit maker does not simply take your measurements once and build to those numbers without further verification. Good makers build fit checkpoints into the commission process — moments where you review elements of the suit before they are fully finished and have a chance to flag fit issues while corrections are still relatively simple to make.

The Head Sizing Check

Before the foam base is covered in fur, a responsible maker sends a photo of the bare foam head alongside clear measurements and often a wearability note — does the head clear your chin and sit comfortably at the back, or does the foam need to be widened? If you are local to a New York area maker, an in-person fitting at this stage is the gold standard for head sizing accuracy. For remote commissions, detailed photos from multiple angles with measurement confirmations allow you to identify any obvious sizing concerns before the much more expensive furring process begins.

The Bodysuit Mockup Stage

Many professional makers create a basic fabric mockup of the bodysuit using inexpensive material before cutting into the final fur. This mockup is sent to you — or worn during an in-person fitting — to verify that the pattern creates the correct fit at all key points. A mockup that binds at the shoulders or pulls across the back when you raise your arms indicates a pattern adjustment is needed before final construction. This stage catches the majority of fit problems before they are built into a finished and irreversible suit.

Building in Wear Ease for New York Events

Accurate measurements are the foundation of good sizing, but experienced makers also build in wear ease — small amounts of additional space at key points that accommodate movement, ventilation, and the thickness of any undersuit layer worn beneath the fursuit. For New York summer events particularly, a maker who understands wear ease builds suits that remain comfortable as body temperature rises and minor swelling of extremities occurs over a long day of wearing. This consideration separates makers experienced with con environments from those who build primarily for static display or short photoshoots.
